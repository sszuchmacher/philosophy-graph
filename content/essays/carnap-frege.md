Carnap had studied under **Frege** at Jena — one of the very few students ever to attend Frege's sparsely populated lectures on the new logic — and he later said Frege's teaching had influenced him more than any other. Through Carnap, Frege's ideas passed directly into the Vienna Circle and the logical-empiricist tradition.

From Frege, Carnap took first of all the **mathematical logic** itself — the quantificational logic Frege invented — which he mastered from its source and made the indispensable instrument of his philosophy. He also absorbed Frege's **logicism**, the thesis that mathematics reduces to logic, which he defended and built into the logical-empiricist account of mathematics as analytic and empty of factual content.

Just as important was Frege's conception of philosophical method: the idea that clarity comes through the **logical analysis of language**, that many philosophical confusions arise from misunderstanding the logical form of our statements, and that a properly constructed formal language could dispel them. Frege's **anti-psychologism** — the insistence that logic and meaning are objective, not facts about the mind — became a cornerstone of Carnap's outlook and of analytic philosophy generally.

Carnap develops these into his own distinctive doctrines. Frege's distinction of **sense and reference** feeds into Carnap's technical work on meaning (his "intension and extension"). And Frege's dream of a logically perfect language becomes Carnap's lifelong project of constructing formal **languages** and studying their "logical syntax" — the view that philosophy's proper business is the logical analysis and construction of the language of science, and that many disputes are really choices between linguistic frameworks.

So Carnap continues Frege as a direct pupil who carried the master's logic, logicism, anti-psychologism, and analytic method from the empty lecture hall at Jena into the center of twentieth-century philosophy. What Frege had created in obscurity — a new logic and a vision of philosophy as logical analysis — Carnap turned into the working program of logical empiricism, transmitting Frege's revolution to the movement that would dominate analytic philosophy of science.
