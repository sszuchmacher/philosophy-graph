In the 1930s and 40s Heidegger lectured for years on Nietzsche, and the massive result is one of the strangest acts of interpretation in modern philosophy: Heidegger reads Nietzsche, the great destroyer of metaphysics, as its final *completion* — the last metaphysician of the West.

Nietzsche had understood himself as the philosopher who overturned the Platonic-Christian tradition: he abolished the "true world" of eternal Forms and God, revalued all values, and proclaimed becoming, will to power, and the eternal return in place of being, God, and the beyond. He thought he had stepped outside metaphysics.

Heidegger insists he had not. Metaphysics, for Heidegger, is the tradition that forgets the question of **Being** itself while asking only about beings — their ground, their supreme instance, their value. And Nietzsche, he argues, remains inside it. To make **will to power** the basic character of all reality is to give one more answer to the old metaphysical question "what is the being of beings?" — will to power is Nietzsche's name for the beingness of beings, successor to Plato's Forms and the medieval God. And the **eternal return** is his account of how beings *are* as a whole. Far from escaping metaphysics, Nietzsche gives its ultimate, self-consuming formulation.

Worse, Heidegger reads Nietzsche's will to power as the philosophical truth of the modern age of **technology** — the reduction of all beings, nature and humanity alike, to resources for the endless enhancement of power. Nietzsche is the thinker who articulates, without quite seeing it, the essence of the technological will to dominate that Heidegger regards as the deepest danger.

This is **reinterpretation** as appropriation and reversal. Heidegger takes Nietzsche's self-understanding — the overcomer of metaphysics — and turns it inside out: Nietzsche is metaphysics reaching its end and exhausting itself, the point at which the forgetting of Being becomes total and, precisely thereby, a new questioning might begin. He honors Nietzsche as a fate to be confronted while denying him the escape he claimed — reading the anti-metaphysician as the one in whom Western metaphysics finally completes and consumes itself.
