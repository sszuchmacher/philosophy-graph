Plotinus is usually cast as the great Platonist, but his system is quietly built with Aristotelian materials, absorbed and reassigned. Neoplatonism is, among other things, a synthesis in which key doctrines of Aristotle are lifted out of Aristotle's own framework and set into the descending hierarchy from the One.

The clearest case is **Intellect**, *Nous*. Aristotle's God is "thought thinking itself" — a self-contemplating intellect, the unmoved mover that draws the cosmos by being loved. Plotinus takes this over almost intact, but *demotes* it. For Aristotle, self-thinking Intellect is the highest reality, the divine. For Plotinus it is only the *second* hypostasis: real and divine, yes, but derivative, because it involves a duality — thought and its object — and whatever is two must come from something prior that is perfectly One. The Aristotelian God becomes a stage on the way down from a source beyond it.

Plotinus also draws on Aristotle's account of intellect grasping its objects by *being* them — knower and known united — to describe how *Nous* contains the Forms not as things outside it but as its own contents, thinking and being coinciding at that level.

The move is characteristic of late-antique philosophy: not choosing between Plato and Aristotle but *ranking* them. Aristotle's self-thinking Intellect is true — as far as it goes — but it is not the summit; above it lies the Platonic One beyond being. By subordinating Aristotelian *Nous* to the ineffable One, Plotinus fuses the two greatest Greek philosophers into one architecture, handing the later tradition a Plato and an Aristotle already reconciled into a single ascending ladder toward God.
