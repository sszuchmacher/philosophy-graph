Plotinus called himself nothing but a faithful interpreter of Plato, six centuries after the master. Yet what he produced — Neoplatonism — is a transformation so profound that it reads almost as a new religion built from Platonic materials.

Plato had scattered suggestions: the Form of the **Good** "beyond being" in the *Republic*, the ascent to Beauty itself in the *Symposium*, the world-making craftsman of the *Timaeus*, the immortal soul. Plotinus gathers these into a single vast descent. From the **One** — utterly simple, beyond being and thought, Plato's Good raised to the absolute — flows, by necessary overflow, the **Intellect** (*Nous*), which contains the Forms; from Intellect flows **Soul**; from Soul, the sensible world. All reality is a graded emanation from a single ineffable source.

This is more systematic than anything in Plato, and more *vertical*: reality is a hierarchy of descending unity, and the philosopher's task is to reverse the flow — to turn inward and upward, through soul and intellect, toward mystical union with the One.

> The One is all things and no one of them.

Plotinus reads even Plato's most difficult dialectical dialogues, like the *Parmenides*, as coded metaphysics of this ascent. Where Plato often argued and left questions open, Plotinus systematizes and mysticizes — the dialogues become scripture, their tensions resolved into a single luminous structure.

The reinterpretation proved world-historical. It is *this* Plato — hierarchical, otherworldly, mystical, straining toward union with the ineffable One — that passes into Augustine and Christian theology, into Islamic and Jewish thought, and shapes how the West would read Plato himself for a thousand years.
