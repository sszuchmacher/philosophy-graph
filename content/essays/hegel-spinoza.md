Hegel said that to be a philosopher one must first be a Spinozist — that Spinoza's system was the indispensable starting point, the great vision of a single infinite reality of which all finite things are moments. His own Absolute inherits Spinoza's monism: there is one whole, and everything is a determination of it.

But Hegel judged Spinoza's system fatally incomplete, and his famous verdict names the flaw. Spinoza had grasped the Absolute only as **substance** — and not yet as **subject**.

> Everything turns on grasping the true not only as substance, but equally as subject.

What does the difference mean? Spinoza's one substance is static and given: a complete, self-identical reality in which the finite modes — individual things, minds — are swallowed up, mere transient effects with no reality of their own. It has no inner development, no self-consciousness, no return to itself. The finite simply vanishes into the infinite; negation and difference are not truly real. Hegel calls this "acosmism" — the world of distinct things is effectively denied.

To grasp the Absolute as **subject** is to grasp it as *self-developing* — as a process that goes out of itself into difference and finitude and returns to itself enriched, achieving self-consciousness through that very movement. The finite is not merely dissolved but is a necessary moment through which the infinite realizes itself. Substance must become subject: the whole must be understood as Spirit coming to know itself through its own othering.

So Hegel radicalizes Spinoza by setting his frozen substance in motion. He keeps the monism — one Absolute, all things its expressions — but insists it is not an inert, geometric substance but a living, self-differentiating, self-knowing process. Negativity, contradiction, and development, which Spinoza's system lacked, become the engine of the whole.

Spinoza gave German Idealism the one infinite reality; Hegel completes the project Schelling had reopened by making that reality a subject — substance that thinks, develops, and returns to itself as Spirit.
