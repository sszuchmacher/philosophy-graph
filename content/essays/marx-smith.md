Marx's *Capital* is subtitled "A Critique of Political Economy," and the political economy he critiques is above all that of Adam Smith and Ricardo. But "critique" does not mean rejection. Marx took the classical economists with immense seriousness, absorbed their science, and then turned their own best tools against the system they had described and largely defended.

The crucial tool he inherits is the **labor theory of value**. Smith had located the real source of a commodity's value in the labor required to produce it — value as, at bottom, congealed human work. Marx seizes this classical doctrine and makes it the foundation of *Capital*. He does not oppose Smith with a rival theory; he takes Smith's own principle and follows it more rigorously than Smith did.

And following it rigorously, Marx argues, exposes what Smith's economics concealed: **exploitation**. If labor is the source of value, where does the capitalist's profit come from? Marx's answer is **surplus value**. The worker sells not their labor but their *labor-power*, for a wage equal to what it costs to sustain them; but in the working day they produce *more* value than that wage — and this surplus, unpaid, is appropriated by the capitalist. Profit is not a reward for enterprise or abstinence but the extraction of unpaid labor. The classical labor theory, consistently applied, reveals the wage relation as exploitation hidden beneath the appearance of a free and equal exchange.

Marx's deeper charge is that Smith and the classical economists mistook the **historical** for the **natural**. They treated capitalism's categories — wage labor, capital, private property, the market — as if they were eternal features of economic life, the natural order of things. Marx historicizes them: these are the specific relations of one passing mode of production, which arose under definite conditions and will pass away.

So Marx reads the classics against themselves. He keeps Smith's science — the labor theory of value, the analysis of the division of labor and accumulation — and uses it to demonstrate the exploitation and the historical limits that Smith's own framework had obscured. Political economy, pushed to consistency, becomes the critique of capital.
