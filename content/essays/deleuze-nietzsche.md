Deleuze's *Nietzsche and Philosophy* (1962) was a landmark — it rescued Nietzsche from the Nazi-tainted and merely "literary" readings and made him a rigorous philosopher of *force* and *affirmation*, and it laid the foundation of Deleuze's own thought. Of all his masters, Nietzsche is the one Deleuze follows most closely.

From Nietzsche, Deleuze takes first the philosophy of **force and will to power**. He reads reality as a play of forces — active and reactive — and the will to power not as a desire for domination but as the differential element that determines the quality of forces, that which produces difference. This becomes the ground of Deleuze's own metaphysics of difference and intensity: being as a field of forces and differential relations rather than stable substances.

Second, and most important, Deleuze takes Nietzsche's **affirmation**. He reads the eternal return not as the literal recurrence of the same but as a principle of *selection and affirmation*: it affirms only difference and becoming, and expels everything reactive and negative. Deleuze makes this the heart of his Nietzscheanism — a philosophy of pure **affirmation** of difference, of saying Yes to becoming, opposed to every philosophy of negation and lack.

This yields Deleuze's sharpest polemic, drawn from Nietzsche: the war on the **dialectic** and on **negation**. Where Hegel makes difference depend on negation and opposition (a thing is what it is by *not* being others, and reality advances by contradiction), Deleuze, following Nietzsche, insists that difference is **positive and primary** — affirmation, not the labor of the negative. Nietzsche is his great weapon against Hegel: the affirmative, differential philosophy against the dialectic of negation and resentment.

Deleuze also inherits Nietzsche's **symptomatology** — the practice of reading thoughts, values, and cultures as symptoms of the forces and modes of life that produce them, evaluating them by whether they express active, life-enhancing forces or reactive, life-denying ones.

So Deleuze continues Nietzsche as his most faithful and creative twentieth-century heir. He takes the philosophy of force and will to power, the affirmation of difference and becoming through the eternal return, the assault on negation and the dialectic, and the diagnostic reading of values as symptoms — and builds them into his own metaphysics of difference. Nietzsche gave Deleuze the affirmative, anti-dialectical philosophy of pure difference that is the signature of all his work.
