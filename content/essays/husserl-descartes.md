Husserl saw himself as a new Descartes. He titled a major work *Cartesian Meditations*, deliberately echoing Descartes's *Meditations*, and presented phenomenology as the fulfillment of the project Descartes had begun but botched: the search for an absolutely certain foundation for all knowledge by turning inward to the thinking self.

From Descartes, Husserl takes the **method of radical doubt** and the move to **subjectivity** as the ground of certainty. Descartes had swept away everything dubitable to reach one indubitable point — the *cogito*, the thinking self — and tried to rebuild knowledge from there. Husserl's *epoché*, the phenomenological "bracketing" of the existence of the world, is a deliberate reprise: suspend all our natural assumptions about whether things really exist, and attend only to what is absolutely given — the field of pure consciousness and its contents. Like Descartes, he seeks apodictic certainty in the self's immediate awareness of its own experience, and makes the ego the starting point of philosophy.

But Husserl thinks Descartes stopped short and stumbled. Descartes reached the *cogito* and then immediately mishandled it — treating the thinking self as a little **substance**, a *res cogitans*, a piece of the world, and rushing to prove God and the external world so as to escape back into ordinary metaphysics. This, Husserl says, betrays the discovery. The transcendental ego revealed by the reduction is not a thing in the world, not a mental substance, but the pure field of consciousness *for which* any world is given. Descartes had the door open and walked through it backwards.

So Husserl continues Descartes by taking up the Cartesian quest for certain foundations in the self, while correcting what he saw as Descartes's fatal error. The *cogito* becomes the transcendental ego; the search for certainty becomes the phenomenological description of pure consciousness; and Descartes's dualism of two substances is set aside for an analysis of consciousness as the site where all meaning and being are constituted. Phenomenology is offered as Cartesianism at last done right.
