John Dewey completed the pragmatist triumvirate, and it was above all James — especially the *Principles of Psychology* — that turned him from his early Hegelian idealism toward the pragmatism he would carry into every corner of American life.

From James, Dewey took the core pragmatist commitments: that thought is to be understood by its function in experience and action, not as a mirror of a ready-made reality; that ideas are instruments for coping with problems rather than copies of things; and that truth is bound up with the practical outcomes of inquiry. James's biological, functional picture of the mind — consciousness as a tool organisms use to adapt and act, the "stream of consciousness" serving the needs of a living creature in its environment — became the seed of Dewey's whole philosophy.

Dewey develops this into **instrumentalism**: the view that concepts, theories, and even logical forms are *instruments* for resolving problematic situations, to be judged by how well they do that work. Thinking begins when a situation becomes troubled, proceeds by forming and testing hypotheses, and ends when the difficulty is resolved and experience flows smoothly again. This is James's functional psychology generalized into a complete theory of **inquiry**.

But Dewey also broadens and socializes what in James remained more individual and psychological. Where James often focused on the personal — the individual's beliefs, religious experience, the will to believe — Dewey turns pragmatism outward, into a public philosophy of **education, democracy, and social reform**. Intelligence, for Dewey, is not just a personal tool but a social method: the experimental, cooperative resolution of shared problems, of which democracy is the political expression and progressive education the training ground.

So Dewey continues James by systematizing and civicizing him. He takes James's functional account of mind and thought and builds it into a comprehensive philosophy in which knowing is a mode of doing, truth is "warranted assertibility" reached through inquiry, and the method of experimental intelligence is extended from the individual's coping to the whole project of democratic life. Pragmatism, personal and psychological in James, becomes in Dewey a program for remaking society.
